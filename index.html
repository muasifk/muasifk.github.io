Biography

Muhammad Asif Khan (SMIEEE) is a Research Scientist at Qatar Mobility Innovations Center (QMIC), Doha, Qatar. He received a Ph.D. degree in electrical engineering from Qatar University (2020), an M.Sc. degree in telecommunication engineering from the University of Engineering and Technology Taxila, Pakistan (2013), and a B.Sc. degree in telecommunication engineering from the University of Engineering and Technology Peshawar, Pakistan (2009). He was awarded the Postdoctoral Research Award (PDRA) from the Qatar National Research Fund (QNRF), for his research work as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Qatar University, Doha, Qatar. He was a Research Mentor at the Qatar University Young Scientists Center (QUYSC). He published over 40 journal articles and conference papers including two book chapters. Dr. Khan is a member of IET and a Chartered Engineer (CEng) with the Engineering Council (UK). He is an associate editor of IEEE Transactions on Consumer Technology (TCE), IEEE Transactions on Technology and Society (TTS), and IEEE Future Directions Technology Policy and Ethics Newsletter. He serves on several IEEE committees including the TCCC, TCPAMI, MDA-TC, MMTC, IOT, and Sensors Council. He also served as a TPC member of numerous IEEE conferences including ICC, Globecom, CCNC, VTC, ICCE, BigData, Sensors, GEM, etc.

Research Interests

Mobile computing, cybersecurity, edge intelligence, IoT, TinyML.
